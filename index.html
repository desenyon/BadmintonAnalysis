Root Causes
Weak defensive clear under pressure. When pinned in the rear court, the player cannot hit a deep flat clear. Opponents intercept at mid-court and attack immediately. A –9.8 avg margin means the opponent wins 3–4 extra points per game through this single pattern.
No net threat. Defaulting to a lift every time at the net lets opponents set up in attack formation. This single habit may account for 5–8 points per game lost.
Footwork recovery speed. The 5-21, 6-21 scoreline vs MV implies the opponent finishes rallies faster than this player can reset. Split-step timing and recovery base are almost certainly the mechanical issue.

Priority Fixes
1
Multi-shuttle rear court drill: 20-min sessions of consecutive smash-drop-clear sequences to build stroke quality and recovery footwork simultaneously.
2
Net kill practice: Daily 10-min net kill drills (tight cross-court, straight, spinning net shots) to force opponents to lift rather than push.
3
Defensive clear under fatigue: Practice deep clears specifically when tired — this is precisely when they break down in matches.

Institute a post-game-win mental reset protocol for all singles players
Both Teams
Evidence across 3 events: Varsity GS3 won G1 (23-21) then lost G3 10-21. JV GS2 won G2 (21-18) then collapsed G3 11-18. JV GD1 won G2 (21-19) vs Fremont then lost G3 16-21. This pattern costs real match points each time it occurs.
The fix: After every won game in practice scrimmages, require a 60-second explicit reset — both players state one thing they will do differently in the next game. The physical act of verbalizing intent before serving rewires the passive "protect the lead" instinct.
Expected impact: Flipping 1–2 of these G3 losses per season would directly change match score from 10-5 to 11-4 or 12-3, which is significant at the end of a season when point differential can matter.
